Master the NYT Letterboxed Puzzle – Daily Strategies & Solutions

Letterboxed is one of the most popular and brain-teasing word games created by The New York Times Games team. It challenges players to create a series of connected words that use all 12 letters arranged around a square — without repeating any letter sides. Sounds simple? It’s not.

According to puzzle game research published in 2025, games like Letterboxed significantly improve cognitive flexibility, memory, and vocabulary recall. That’s why educators, language coaches, and even psychologists recommend daily word puzzles to keep the brain sharp.
